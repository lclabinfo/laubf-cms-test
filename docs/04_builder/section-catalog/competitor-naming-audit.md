# Competitor Section Naming Audit

> **Date:** 2026-03-19
> **Purpose:** Validate our proposed 24 section names against 5 major website builders. Inform naming decisions for church admin users (non-technical, low design confidence).
> **Builders audited:** Squarespace 7.1, Shopify (Dawn + Horizon), Framer, Webflow/Relume, Wix

---

## Part 1: Hero / Page Header — The Big Naming Question

This is the most contested naming area. Here's what each builder does:

| Builder | Homepage top section | Inner page top section | Term for navigation bar |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Squarespace** | No special type — first section IS the hero. Often uses "About" or "Headlines" category layouts | Same as homepage — no distinction | Header (structural) |
| **Shopify Dawn** | "Image banner" | Same — no distinction | Header (structural) |
| **Shopify Horizon** | "Hero" (under "Banners" category) | Same Hero section, any page | Header (structural) |
| **Framer** | "Hero" | Same Hero, any page | "Header" (means navbar) |
| **Webflow/Relume** | "Hero Header" (28 variants) | "Header" (100+ variants, simpler/utilitarian) | "Navbar" |
| **Wix** | "Welcome" | Same Welcome, any page | Header (structural) |

### Key Findings

1. **Only Webflow explicitly separates "Hero" from "Page Header."** They use "Hero Header" for dramatic homepage sections and "Header" for utilitarian inner-page tops. Everyone else treats the hero as a general-purpose section usable on any page.

2. **"Hero" is the most common term** (Shopify Horizon, Framer, Webflow all use it). But Squarespace avoids it entirely, and Wix calls it "Welcome."

3. **For non-technical users, "Hero" is jargon.** Wix's "Welcome" and Squarespace's approach (no special name) are more accessible. Shopify Dawn's "Image banner" is the most literal/descriptive.

### Recommendation Options for Our Builder

| Option | Homepage Section | Inner Page Section | Ministry Section | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A: Webflow model** | "Hero" | "Page Header" | "Ministry Header" | Clear separation. Most precise. But "Hero" is jargon for pastors. |
| **B: Shopify Horizon model** | "Hero" (all pages) | Same "Hero" with variants | "Ministry Header" | Simple — one type for all top-of-page. But doesn't distinguish homepage drama from inner page utility. |
| **C: Wix-inspired** | "Welcome" | "Page Header" | "Ministry Header" | "Welcome" is warmer and more intuitive for church context. Clear separation. |
| **D: Descriptive/literal** | "Banner" | "Page Header" | "Ministry Header" | Avoids all jargon. "Banner" = big visual section. Shopify Dawn precedent. |
| **E: Church-optimized** | "Welcome Banner" | "Page Header" | "Ministry Header" | Combines warmth of "Welcome" with descriptive "Banner." Most accessible for pastors. |

### Category: Content

| # | Our Proposed Name | Squarespace | Shopify Horizon | Framer | Webflow/Relume | Wix |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | Image & Text | (Details category) | Media with text / Image with text | Features | Feature Sections | Services / About |
| 4 | Quote | Quotes | Pull quote | Testimonials | Testimonial Sections | Testimonials |
| 5 | Call to Action | (no specific type) | Email signup / Promotion | CTA | CTA Sections | Promotion / Subscribe |
| 6 | About | About | Rich text | About | Long Form Content | About |
| 7 | Statement | (no equivalent) | Rich text | (no equivalent) | (no equivalent) | Text |
| 8 | Photo Gallery | Images | (no equivalent) | Gallery | Gallery Sections | Gallery |

**Observations:**
- "Image & Text" maps to Shopify's "Media with text" and "Image with text." Both are clearer than our current "MEDIA_TEXT." Shopify's "Image with text" is the most plain-English option.
- "Quote" — Most builders put this under "Testimonials." Consider whether our quote section is really a testimonial (social proof) or a decorative text treatment. If it's for Bible verses / inspirational quotes (church context), "Quote" is the better name.
- "Call to Action" — Framer and Webflow use "CTA." Shopify calls it "Email signup." Wix says "Promotion" or "Subscribe." For pastors, "Call to Action" spelled out is clearest.
- "Statement" has no direct equivalent anywhere. It's a unique scroll-tracked paragraph section. Consider renaming to "Rich Text" (Shopify), "Text" (Wix), or keeping "Statement" if the visual treatment is distinctive enough.
- "Photo Gallery" — Universal term. "Gallery" alone works too. Every builder has this.

### Category: Cards

| # | Our Proposed Name | Squarespace | Shopify Horizon | Framer | Webflow/Relume | Wix |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 9 | Card Grid | Features | Icons with text / Multicolumn | Features / Bento | Feature Sections | Services |

| 12 | Welcome Banner | (no equivalent) | (no equivalent) | (no equivalent) | (no equivalent) | Welcome |

**Observations:**
- "Card Grid" and "Feature List" both map to "Features" in most builders. Consider whether these are different enough to stay separate. Shopify Horizon distinguishes "Icons with text" (icon cards) from "Multicolumn" (general grid). Our split into "Card Grid" (image vs icon variants) and "Feature List" (acronym-style) seems justified.
- "Alternating Content" — Shopify Dawn calls this "Multirow." Shopify Horizon calls it "Media with text." "Alternating Content" is more descriptive of the layout than either. Could also be "Zigzag" or "Content Rows."
- "Welcome Banner" — Only Wix has something similar ("Welcome" category). This is a church-specific newcomer section. Name is clear enough.

**Observations:**
- "Content Listing" — Good generic name. Maps to Shopify's "Featured collection" / "Product list" pattern. Our approach of one unified "Content Listing" with a data source dropdown is more consolidated than any competitor.
- "Featured Content" — Shopify uses "Featured" prefix for curated subsets. Our name aligns well.
- "Upcoming Events" and "Event Calendar" — These are church-specific. No builder separates event listing from event calendar as distinct section types. Consider whether they should merge.
- "Recurring Meetings" — Fully church/organization-specific. No competitor equivalent. Name is clear for the target user.

### Notable Category Naming Patterns

- **Framer** uses function names: Hero, Features, Pricing, FAQ, CTA
- **Webflow** uses content-type names: Hero Header Sections, Feature Sections, Team Sections
- **Wix** uses relationship names: Welcome, About, Services, Contact, Team

## Part 4: Church-Specific Sections — Are They Too Specific?

Reviewing sections flagged as potentially too church-specific:

| Section | Church-Specific? | Generic Alternative Name | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ministry Header | Yes — "ministry" is church terminology | "Group Header" or "Department Header" | Keep "Ministry" for MVP. Rename if expanding to non-church orgs. |
| Ministry Intro | Yes | "Group Intro" or "Department" | Same as above. |
| Recurring Meetings | Somewhat — meetings are universal | Keep as-is | Already generic enough. Bible studies, small groups are the content, not the section name. |
| Welcome Banner | Somewhat — "welcome" is churchy | Keep as-is | Also used by Wix for their hero sections. Not church-specific. |
| Content Listing (sermons) | The section is generic; the data source (sermons) is church-specific | Keep as-is | Section name is generic. Data sources are church-specific by design. |

**Bottom line:** Only 2 sections use church-specific language in their names: Ministry Header and Ministry Intro. Everything else is generic. This is a good balance — the section *names* are accessible, while the *content* within them is church-specific.

## Part 5: Summary Recommendations

### Naming Changes to Consider

| # | Current Proposed Name | Suggested Change | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hero | "Banner" or "Welcome" | Less jargon for pastors. See Option D or E in Part 1. |
| — | (new) | Add "Page Header" as separate type | Webflow precedent. Separates homepage drama from inner-page utility. |
| 4 | Quote | Keep "Quote" (not "Testimonial") | Church context = Bible verses, not customer reviews. |
| 7 | Statement | Consider "Rich Text" or "Text Block" | No competitor has "Statement." It's unclear what it does from the name alone. |
| 11 | Alternating Content | Consider "Content Rows" or keep | "Alternating Content" is descriptive but wordy. |
| 19 | Location | Consider "Location & Schedule" | More descriptive of what the variants contain. |

### Hero/Header Split — Final Options

Based on all competitor research and the church admin persona:

**Recommended: Option E (Church-optimized) with page header split**

| Type | Name | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage hero | **Welcome Banner** | Top of homepage. Big, dramatic, welcoming. |
| Inner page header | **Page Header** | Top of any non-home page. Simple heading + optional image. |
| Ministry page header | **Ministry Header** | Top of ministry hub pages. Specialized with social links. |

This gives you 3 clear, non-overlapping types instead of trying to merge 5 into 1 with confusing variants. Each name tells the pastor exactly when to use it.
